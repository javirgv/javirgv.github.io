## Overview

During the UCD Academy observing run at Calar Alto Observatory, I contributed to optical observations of the gamma-ray burst GRB 260310A / AT2026fgk using the CAHA 1.23m telescope.

The observations were part of a target-of-opportunity follow-up campaign and contributed to results reported in NASA GCN Circular 43979.

## Observational context

Gamma-ray bursts are among the most energetic transient events in the Universe. Rapid optical follow-up observations are important because their afterglows fade quickly and can provide information about the burst environment, host galaxy, and emission properties.

For this campaign, the optical counterpart candidate of GRB 260310A / AT2026fgk was observed using the CAHA 1.23m telescope equipped with the DLR-MkIII CCD camera. The observations included BVR-band imaging.
