## Overview

This project was carried out as a summer research student in the UCD Star Formation Research Group under the supervision of Dr Rebeca García López.

The project focused on the protostellar jets of the T Tauri star DG Tau, using ESO ERIS/VLT integral-field spectroscopic data to study emission features, jet kinematics, and layered velocity structure.

## Scientific motivation

Protostellar jets are linked to the early stages of star formation and accretion. Studying their structure and kinematics helps constrain physical models of how young stars interact with their surrounding disks and launch outflows.

DG Tau is a well-studied T Tauri star with prominent jet emission, making it a useful target for analysing jet morphology, velocity structure, and comparisons with magnetohydrodynamic disk-wind models.

## Data and methods

The project involved working with ESO ERIS/VLT IFU FITS data.

The analysis included:

- Inspecting and handling spectroscopic data cubes.
- Identifying emission features in the jet.
- Analysing spatial and velocity structure.
- Producing maps and visualisations of emission and kinematic properties.
- Comparing observational results with theoretical disk-wind models.
- Presenting results to academic audiences.
