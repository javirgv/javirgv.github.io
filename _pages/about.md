I am a final-year BSc Physics with Astronomy & Space Science student at University College Dublin, with a structured elective in Programming. My interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, scientific computing, astronomical data analysis, and software-oriented research.

My final-year project focuses on **galaxy morphology classification using convolutional neural networks**. I am training models on SDSS and Galaxy Zoo 2 data and testing them on smaller ground-based telescope observations, with the aim of understanding how well machine-learning methods transfer across different astronomical imaging conditions. This work involves Python, TensorFlow/Keras, NumPy, Pandas, AstroPy, scikit-learn, data preprocessing, model evaluation, and reproducible project organisation.

I have also worked on astrophysical research using **ESO/VLT ERIS spectroscopic data** of protostellar jets, where I developed Python-based analysis workflows for observational astronomy data. This experience strengthened my interest in applying computational methods to real scientific datasets.

Alongside astronomy and physics, I am interested in broader software engineering, machine learning, data science, and computational science roles. I enjoy practical problem-solving, building clear analysis pipelines, and using code to extract insight from complex datasets.

This website collects my academic background, technical projects, research experience, and selected GitHub repositories. You can find my code and project work on [GitHub](https://github.com/javirgv).
